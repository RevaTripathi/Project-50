CAMEL

  1. A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back.
  2. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons or chamaeleons are a distinctive and highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202 species described as of June 2015.
  2. These species come in a range of colors, and many species have the ability to change color.

Cicada

  1. The cicadas are a superfamily, the Cicadoidea, of insects in the order Hemiptera.
  2. They are in the suborder Auchenorrhyncha, along with smaller jumping bugs such as leafhoppers and froghoppers.
